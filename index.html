3 · Inlay Pair Finder

For inlay work the hole and the plug are cut from the same template and their offsets must be equal. The hole uses offset (B−C)/2, the plug uses (B+C)/2 — so you need two different setups that give the same offset.

5 · How the maths works

The guide bush rides against the template edge while the cutter spins inside it. The cut line is therefore offset from the template edge. Only two formulas exist:

  • Offset = (Bush − Cutter) / 2 — when the piece you keep is on the same side as the bush (female→hole, male→plug).
  • Offset = (Bush + Cutter) / 2 — when the cutter works beyond the bush footprint (female→plug, male→hole).

Corners: a round cutter can't cut a sharp internal corner — the minimum internal corner radius equals the cutter radius. Likewise the round bush can't follow template corners tighter than the bush radius, so give your templates corner radii of at least half the bush diameter.

Depth of cut: rule of thumb — don't take more than about half the cutter diameter per pass in hardwood (a full diameter in soft material with a sharp cutter). The calculator suggests a pass count when you enter a cut depth.

Worked example (from the source chart): a 60 mm circular template with an 8 mm offset pair. Hole: 8 mm cutter + 24 mm bush → offset (24−8)/2 = 8 → hole Ø = 60 − 2×8 = 44 mm. Plug: 6 mm cutter + 10 mm bush → offset (10+6)/2 = 8 → plug Ø = 60 − 2×8 = 44 mm. A perfect-fit inlay.
